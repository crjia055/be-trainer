Pitch & Banks
15 phrases from the pitch room and syndicate desk — with daily & business examples
Done: 0  |  Fluent: 0
Pitch
"Our desk is reading the tape in real-time."
›
What it means
Your team is monitoring live market flow and data to judge market conditions as they happen — not from a model, but from what is actually trading right now. It implies active, experienced market surveillance.
💼 Business
"Our desk is reading the tape in real-time — we know exactly when the market is receptive and when to wait for the volatility to settle."Pitch to issuer on timing advantage
"We're reading the tape closely this week — there's a window opening in the CB market and we want to make sure you're positioned to take it."Proactive client call
🗣️ Daily
"I've been reading the tape on the job market lately — salaries in our sector are moving fast and I think it's worth having a conversation."Negotiating a raise
"She's been reading the tape on the neighbourhood for months — she knew exactly when prices had peaked before anyone else did."Discussing a smart property buyer
💡 Originally a trading-floor expression — the 'tape' was the ticker tape printing live stock prices. Now means any real-time monitoring of signals or data. Use it when you want to claim an edge based on live intelligence rather than modelled projections.
"We don't just execute — we manage the narrative."
›
What it means
Execution is the mechanics — getting the deal done. Managing the narrative is controlling how the deal is perceived and communicated. The argument is that the story around a transaction matters as much as the transaction itself.
💼 Business
"We don't just execute — we manage the narrative. We ensure the equity story is framed in a way that resonates with both credit and equity investors."Pitch differentiation
"Anyone can execute a refinancing. The question is how you manage the narrative with the rating agencies and the press at the same time."Strategy discussion with CFO
🗣️ Daily
"He didn't just resign — he managed the narrative. The announcement framed it as his decision on his timeline, and it landed completely differently."Discussing a colleague's career move
"You need to manage the narrative with the kids before they hear the news from someone else."Conversation about a family situation
💡 'Manage the narrative' = control how something is perceived and communicated. One of the most powerful phrases in professional English because it names something real that most people don't articulate.
"We have skin in the game right alongside you."
›
What it means
The bank has a genuine stake in the outcome — not just earning a fee, but taking on real risk or exposure. The phrase directly addresses the client's concern that advisors profit regardless of outcome.
💼 Business
"We have skin in the game right alongside you — we're providing a hard underwrite on this. We're putting our own balance sheet on the line."Pitch closing — underwrite commitment
"This isn't a situation where we advise and walk away. We have skin in the game — our own capital is in this fund alongside yours."Fund pitch to LP
🗣️ Daily
"I'm not just advising you — I have skin in the game here too. If this goes wrong, it affects my team directly."Reassuring a colleague of your commitment
"He has real skin in the game now — he put his own savings into the restaurant, not just his time."Discussing a friend's new business
💡 Originally from gambling — you can't win if you haven't bet. In any context it means: my interests are fully aligned with yours because I'm taking the same risk. One of the most credible statements you can make in a pitch.
"This deal is top-of-mind for our senior leadership."
›
What it means
The transaction has the active attention of the most senior people in the organisation — the client is not just a number being handled at the junior level.
💼 Business
"This deal is top-of-mind for our senior leadership — the Head of Asia ECM is personally tracking this alongside me."Pitch — signalling senior attention
"I want you to know this mandate is top-of-mind for us at the highest level. You'll have direct access to the team throughout."Onboarding a new client
🗣️ Daily
"The school's Ofsted visit is top-of-mind for every teacher right now — you can feel it in the building."Describing a stressful period at work
"The trip is top-of-mind for me this week — I keep thinking about what to pack."Casual conversation about an upcoming holiday
💡 'Top-of-mind' = the most immediate, active concern — what someone is actively thinking about right now. The phrase tells the client: you are being thought about, not filed away.
"We aren't looking for a quick fee — we want to support your capital structure for the long haul."
›
What it means
Explicitly rejecting the transactional model — the bank wants a relationship across the lifecycle of the client's financing, not just a one-time advisory fee.
💼 Business
"We aren't looking for a quick fee. We want to support your capital structure for the long haul — from this issuance through to the final refinancing."Pitch closing
"We're not here for a one-off mandate. We want to be the bank that's with you for the long haul as you scale through the next three capital events."Growth-stage client pitch
🗣️ Daily
"I'm not asking to fix this once and move on — I want to help you manage it for the long haul."Offering sustained support to a friend
"They aren't looking for a quick sale. They want tenants who'll stay for the long haul and look after the place."Describing a landlord's priorities
💡 'Long haul' = the long term, with a sense of endurance and sustained commitment. Naming 'quick fee' directly — then rejecting it — is what makes this phrase land. It shows you know what clients fear.
Banks / Syndicate
"Let's calibrate our views against the recent comps."
›
What it means
Before going to market or to the client, compare your pricing assumptions against recent comparable transactions to make sure your read is accurate.
💼 Business
"Let's calibrate our views against the recent comps to ensure we aren't misreading the appetite before the pricing call."Pre-call prep between co-leads
"I want to calibrate our premium assumption against the last three deals — I don't want us going in wide and losing credibility."Internal structuring meeting
🗣️ Daily
"Before we set the asking price, let's calibrate against what similar flats on this street have actually sold for."Property discussion
"Let me calibrate my expectations before the interview — what are people at that level actually earning right now?"Job search conversation
💡 'Calibrate' = adjust your view against a fixed reference point to ensure accuracy. More precise than 'compare' — it implies a methodical correction, not just a casual look.
"We need to speak with one voice."
›
What it means
When multiple banks or parties are presenting to a client together, they must deliver a single, consistent message — no contradictions, no visible disagreements.
💼 Business
"We need to speak with one voice during the pricing call this afternoon — any disagreement between us stays in this room."Pre-call sync with co-lead
"I suggest we speak with one voice and propose a range of 25–30%. We've sanity-checked this with our syndicate and it feels like the market-clearing price."Inter-bank alignment
🗣️ Daily
"The teachers need to speak with one voice on this policy — if parents hear different things from different staff it creates confusion."School management meeting
"Before we talk to my parents about the wedding date, let's make sure we're speaking with one voice."Planning conversation with a partner
💡 The rule in syndication: disagreements between banks must be resolved before the client hears anything. The call is not the place to discover you have different views.
"Let's ensure we're aligned on the narrative regarding the use of proceeds."
›
What it means
Before presenting to investors, all banks and the issuer must be telling the exact same story about why the money is being raised and what it will be used for.
💼 Business
"Let's ensure we're aligned on the narrative regarding the use of proceeds before the roadshow — the growth story needs to be consistent across every meeting."Pre-roadshow alignment
"Are we aligned on the narrative? Investors will ask about the capex allocation and I want to make sure we're all saying the same thing."Deal preparation
🗣️ Daily
"Let's make sure we're aligned on the narrative before the team announcement — I don't want people hearing different versions of why we made this hire."Internal communication planning
"Are we aligned on the story we're telling about the renovation? I want to make sure the architect, the builder and us are all saying the same thing to the council."Building project conversation
💡 'Aligned on the narrative' = everyone telling the same story consistently. 'Use of proceeds' is a specific CB/bond term for how the money raised will be spent — always a key investor question.
"Let's take this offline and hash it out."
›
What it means
Remove a difficult or circular debate from the current meeting and resolve it privately — rather than letting the disagreement become visible to the client or a wider group.
💼 Business
"I think we're in a bit of a circular debate here. Let's take this offline and have our respective heads of syndicate hash it out."Deadlocked inter-bank meeting
"We need to hash out the final terms of the contract before we sign anything — let's schedule an hour before the call."Pre-signing prep
🗣️ Daily
"Let's not debate this in front of the whole team — take it offline and hash it out between yourselves."Managing a team disagreement
"We need to hash out the guest list before we send anything — there are some complicated family dynamics to work through."Wedding planning
💡 'Hash it out' = a detailed, sometimes difficult conversation until a resolution is reached. 'Take it offline' = move it out of the current meeting into a private channel. Both are essential for managing visible conflict professionally.
"Let's strip away the noise — the primary objective is execution certainty."
›
What it means
Cut through the irrelevant detail or marginal debates and refocus on what actually matters for the deal to succeed — in this case, getting it done rather than optimising basis points.
💼 Business
"Let's strip away the noise for a second — the primary objective is execution certainty. If we argue over 5 bps and lose the window, we all lose."Breaking a syndicate deadlock
"Strip away the noise on this — the client needs three things: speed, confidentiality, and certainty. Everything else is secondary."Refocusing a project team
🗣️ Daily
"Strip away the noise — you got the job, you like the team, and the salary works. Stop overthinking it."Helping a friend make a decision
"Let's strip away the noise on this flat. The location is perfect and the price is right — the rest we can fix."Property decision
💡 'Strip away the noise' = remove everything irrelevant and focus on the essential signal. Extremely effective for breaking deadlocks because it reframes the debate from 'who is right' to 'what actually matters'.
"If we triangulate the feedback from our respective desks, the sweet spot is around 2.75%."
›
What it means
Combine feedback from multiple independent sources to arrive at a more accurate market-clearing price than any single desk's view could provide alone.
💼 Business
"If we triangulate the feedback from our respective desks, the sweet spot is actually closer to a 2.75% coupon — I suggest we propose a range of 2.50–2.75%."Inter-bank pricing alignment
"Let me triangulate across the three accounts I spoke to this morning before we commit to a number."Pre-pricing market check
🗣️ Daily
"I triangulated across three estate agents and they all came back with the same range — I'm confident in the valuation now."Property selling decision
"I like to triangulate before I make a decision — I ask my doctor, read the research, and talk to someone who's been through it."Making an important personal decision
💡 'Triangulate' = use three or more reference points to locate a more accurate answer than any single source allows. The word sounds analytical and rigorous — it elevates a market view from opinion to method.
"We should stress-test the assumption that the market will absorb a 7-year maturity."
›
What it means
Rigorously examine whether a structural assumption about the deal holds under adverse or realistic conditions — rather than accepting it at face value.
💼 Business
"We should stress-test the assumption that the market will absorb a 7-year maturity right now — our desk is seeing more traction in the 5-year space."Challenging a co-lead's structural assumption
"Before we go to the client with this, let's stress-test the revenue assumptions in the model — they feel optimistic given the current environment."Pre-pitch internal review
🗣️ Daily
"Let's stress-test the plan — what happens if the venue cancels two weeks before? Do we have a backup?"Event planning conversation
"I want to stress-test my assumptions about the move before committing — I've only ever visited in summer."Decision about relocating to a new city
💡 'Stress-test' = examine how something holds up under pressure or adverse conditions. It doesn't say 'you're wrong' — it says 'let's make sure we're right'. Professional, constructive, and precise.
"Once we've squared away our disparities, we can socialise a consensus view with the CFO."
›
What it means
Once the banks have resolved their internal differences, they can then share a unified position with the client — in an informal, preparatory way before the formal meeting.
💼 Business
"Once we've squared away our disparities, we can socialise a consensus view with the CFO before the formal pricing call."Inter-bank meeting
"Let's square away the internal disagreement first — then we present a single view. We never socialise conflicting numbers to the same client."Managing a junior analyst
🗣️ Daily
"Once we've squared away the budget, I can start socialising the idea with the rest of the family."Planning a family holiday
"I need to square this away with my manager before I socialise it with the wider team."Proposing a new initiative at work
💡 Two key phrases: 'square away' = settle and resolve cleanly. 'Socialise' (in business) = share informally to build awareness or buy-in before a formal decision. Both are very high-frequency in professional English.
"We are in lockstep with the co-leads and pacing toward a launch on Thursday."
›
What it means
All banks involved are fully coordinated and moving at the same pace toward a target launch date — no gaps, no misalignment.
💼 Business
"We are in lockstep with the co-leads and pacing toward a launch on Thursday morning — everything is on track."Deal update to the issuer
"The legal team and the syndicate are in lockstep on the timeline — we don't foresee any slippage at this stage."Execution update call
🗣️ Daily
"The two departments are finally in lockstep on the project — it's been a long time coming."Describing improved internal coordination
"We're in lockstep on the renovation plan now — same vision, same budget, same timeline."Couple discussing a home project
💡 'Lockstep' = moving in perfect coordination, like soldiers marching in step. 'Pacing toward' = on track, moving at the right speed. Both signal confidence and coordination — very reassuring words to use in a client update.
"Does that align with your read, or do we need to double-click on the peer comps?"
›
What it means
Check whether the other bank agrees with your market read — and if not, suggest drilling deeper into the comparable transactions together to resolve the disagreement.
💼 Business
"I suggest a range of 25–30%. We've sanity-checked this with our syndicate and it feels like the market-clearing price. Does that align with your read, or do we need to double-click on the peer comps again?"Inter-bank alignment before client call
"Does that align with your view on demand, or should we double-click on the anchor feedback before we go firm?"Pre-pricing calibration
🗣️ Daily
"Does that align with what your doctor said, or do you want to double-click on the second opinion before deciding?"Supporting a friend with a medical decision
"Does that align with your read on the neighbourhood, or should we double-click on the school catchment areas before we make an offer?"Property decision
💡 'Double-click' (in business English) = go one level deeper into a specific area. From the computer metaphor of clicking to expand detail. 'Align with your read' = match what you are seeing or concluding. Both are very current and natural in finance.
